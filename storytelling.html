Bank of England Speech Sentiment & Predictive Modelling

OBJECTIVE

The project aimed to assess the impact of Bank of England (BoE) speech sentiment on key economic indicators. Specifically, it explored whether central bank communication sentiment helps predict financial market movements, and how sentiment can be incorporated into predictive models to support clearer, more effective monetary policy communication.

DATA

Datasets for the project were engineered by combining web-scraped speeches, acquired economic indicators, and publicly available speeches from Kaggle:

  • Speech Data: 1,365 BoE speeches (1998–2025), scraped and cleaned (author corrections, standardised references, duplicates removed).
  • Economic Indicators: GfK Consumer Confidence, CPI, inflation expectations, interest rates, and others, cleaned and reshaped to align with monthly and daily frequencies.
  • Manual Labelling: 59 speeches hand-labelled to benchmark sentiment models.

OVERVIEW

Approach (Python-based):
Python was used throughout for its NLP, data processing, and machine learning capabilities.

  • Key libraries: pandas, numpy, matplotlib, scikit-learn, statsmodels, xgboost, nltk, transformers (HuggingFace), pmdarima
  • Steps: web scraping (BoE speeches), data cleaning and engineering, sentiment model benchmarking (TextBlob, VADER, FinBERT, ChatGPT, CentralBankRoBERTa).
  • Predictive modelling: MLR (GfK confidence), gradient boosting (CPI), SARIMAX (CPI with exogenous sentiment features).
  • Model Selection: CentralBankRoBERTa chosen for domain specificity, agent-specific sentiment, and reproducibility.

Findings and Recommendations
Sentiment adds predictive value:

  • Agent-specific sentiment (e.g. “households”, “firms”) improved prediction accuracy across models.
  • In MLR, lagged sentiment improved GfK consumer confidence prediction (R² = 0.78).
  • In SARIMAX, agent-specific sentiment outperformed average sentiment in predicting CPI (R² = 0.65 vs 0.16).
  • Gradient Boosting identified “households” sentiment lag as highly influential for CPI forecasts.

Lag structure matters:

  • Lagging sentiment by 1–6 months improved predictive accuracy depending on the model and indicator.

Communication matters:

  • Sentiment impacts markets independently of policy changes, supporting the role of forward guidance.

Recommendations:

  • Integrate agent-specific sentiment features into MLR, GB, and SARIMAX models.
  • Use both lagged and contemporaneous sentiment to inform predictive analytics.
  • Recalibrate models regularly to reflect structural shifts in macroeconomic conditions.
  • Explore advanced models to better capture uncertainty and structural change.
  • Align BoE communications with sentiment insights to target specific audiences more effectively, enhancing policy transmission and public trust.

Loyalty Trends for Sales Improvement

OBJECTIVE

TurtleGames, a global retailer of entertainment products, sought a comprehensive analysis of its customer and sales data to understand loyalty trends and improve sales performance. The project aimed to uncover insights into customer behaviour, loyalty point allocation, and brand sentiment using data-driven techniques.

DATA

A dataset of 2,000 observations was analysed, covering:

  • customer demographics,
  • loyalty points,
  • purchase behaviour,
  • and reviews.

Data was cleaned, processed, and checked for missing values, duplicates, and outliers. Exploratory analysis confirmed gender balance (1,120 women, 880 men), educational distribution, and spending patterns. Sentiment analysis of product reviews revealed an overall positive attitude (mean sentiment 0.65).

OVERVIEW

Approach:

  • Imported data into Python (Jupyter Notebook) and R for analysis.
  • Conducted exploratory and statistical analysis using Pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, and R libraries.
  • Applied Multiple Linear Regression (MLR) to identify key drivers of loyalty points (annual income and spending score).
  • Conducted clustering to identify customer segments.
  • Used Natural Language Processing (NLP) for sentiment analysis on product reviews.

Findings and Recommendations:

  • Regression analysis revealed that annual income and spending score were the strongest predictors of loyalty points.
  • Cluster analysis identified five distinct customer groups for targeted marketing.
  • Sentiment analysis showed overall positive sentiment but recommended focused surveys to gain deeper insights.
  • Recommend using the MLR model to tailor loyalty offers based on customer income and spending score.
  • Suggest marketing strategies to retain lower-income customers through engagement incentives and to promote high-value purchases for higher-income customers.
